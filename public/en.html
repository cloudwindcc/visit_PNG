Discover Papua New Guinea

Your Ultimate Travel Guide to Paradise

Explore 10 breathtaking attractions with detailed guides and insider tips

Kokoda Track

96km historic hiking trail - Follow the footsteps of WWII heroes through the Owen Stanley Range. This challenging trek offers incredible views and profound historical significance.

Explore Kokoda Track

Port Moresby Nature Park

30-acre wildlife sanctuary - Home to 550+ native species including tree kangaroos, birds of paradise, and cassowaries. Perfect for wildlife photography and family visits.

Visit Nature Park

Mount Tavurvur / Rabaul

Active Pacific volcano - Witness the raw power of nature where the "Pearl of the Pacific" was buried in 1994. Explore the ghost town and climb the active crater.

Experience Tavurvur

Sepik River

Lifeblood of PNG culture - Navigate 1,126km of pristine river systems, discover 300+ tribal groups, and experience world-renowned wood carving traditions.

Explore Sepik River

Mount Wilhelm

4,509m Oceania's rooftop - Challenge yourself on PNG's highest peak. Experience diverse ecosystems from rainforest to alpine meadows with 360° panoramic views.

Conquer Wilhelm

Goroka / Asaro Mudmen

Living cultural museum - Meet the legendary Asaro Mudmen, witness their mysterious mud masks and ceremonial dances in the cool highlands of Goroka.

Meet the Mudmen

Kimbe Bay

Coral Triangle crown jewel - Dive in 900+ fish species and 400+ coral varieties. World-class diving with 30-40m visibility in pristine tropical waters.

Dive Kimbe Bay

Kuk Early Agricultural Site

UNESCO World Heritage - Explore 7,000-10,000 years of independent agricultural origins. See ancient drainage systems and learn about humanity's farming roots.

Discover Kuk Site

Tari Valley / Huli Wigmen

Iconic tribal culture - Experience the famous Huli Wigmen with their elaborate human hair wigs and vibrant body paint. A living human museum experience.

Visit Huli Wigmen

Alotau / Milne Bay

Pacific War turning point - Dive WWII wrecks and coral reefs in the historic bay where Allied forces first defeated Japan on land. Experience tropical paradise with deep history.

Explore Milne Bay
